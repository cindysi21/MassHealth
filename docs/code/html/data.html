DATA

    The data that was given is a collection of patient health data and medical history. The patients are all residents of Massachusetts, making it safe to assume that the data comes from hospitals all located in Massachusetts. There are 11 datasets in total: 9 pertaining to patient medical records and 2 related to the demographics of Massachusetts that can be used to estimate a patient's economic situation.

    The 9 medical record datasets cover a breadth of patient information, including, but not limited to, immunization history, medication prescriptions, hospital visit history, and results of checkups (Height, BMI, Cholesterol, Blood Pressure…). However, despite the breadth of categories covered, it is not comprehensive. Most sets have missing information that can not be inferred while others have missing information that can actually be found in other datasets. For example, the two datasets, conditions.csv and encounters.csv, possess the same column of data (DESCRIPTION & REASONDESCRIPTION), yet encounters.csv has 1,252 cells with missing information that can be found in conditions.csv. When running regressions, data with depth rather than breadth is most important, so relationships can be drawn from the entire dataset rather than a specific (not usually random) subset.


    This data visualizations best describes the data given. It can be used to reveal relationships between different encounter characteristics or to gain a simple and quick understanding of the data.

OBSERVATIONS (made on uncleaned data)

Patient Observations

  • Age symmetric: Mean = 50, Median = 51, Skewness = -0.011
  • Gender representation: 49% female and 51% male
  • Racial makeup: 74% hispanic, 11% white, 9% black, and 6% asian.
  • Economic Situation: 40.3% list zip codes with median household incomes below the low income threshold

Encounter Observations

  • Most visits were for check-ups (Child: 170258001, Adult: 185349003) or scheduled encounters (Procedure: 308335008, Symptom: 185345009)
  • These 4/28 categories compose 67% of encounters
  • Based off of my understanding of emergency visit (explained in Assumptions), 4.8% of encounters were emergency visits.
  • Records date as far back as 1920 and as recent as 2017. Our scope is 2008-2016 and 88% of the data lies in those 9 years.
